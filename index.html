⚠️ Squat Setup for Your Build

Your long femurs mean standard squat cues won't work for you. Use these modifications every time you squat today:
  • Stance: Wider than shoulder width, toes pointed out 30–45°
  • Heels: Put 5 lb plates under your heels OR wear squat shoes — this makes up for limited ankle mobility with long legs
  • Forward lean: Some torso lean is NORMAL for you. Don't try to stay perfectly upright — that's for short-femur guys
  • Depth: Go to parallel or just below. Don't force ATG until mobility improves
  • Alternative: If barbell squats still feel off, swap to Box Squats (sit back to a bench) — they're built for long-legged lifters

Your Build & What It Means

How your proportions shape your training — and how to use them to your advantage

Body Analysis — 6'0", 140 lbs

Based on your photos, here's what matters for your training:

🦿 Long Femurs (Thigh Bones)

Your legs make up a large proportion of your height. This is the #1 reason squats feel awkward for you.
  • What happens: Long femurs force your torso to lean forward more to keep the barbell over your center of gravity. Your knees also travel further forward.
  • The fix: Wider stance (outside shoulder width), toes pointed out 30–45°, and heel elevation (plates or squat shoes). This shortens the effective lever arm and lets you stay more upright.
  • Box squats are your best friend. Sit back to a box/bench at parallel — this teaches you to load your hips instead of fighting your knee position.
  • Front squats & goblet squats may actually feel easier than back squats since the front-loaded weight naturally counterbalances your torso lean.
  • Don't compare your squat to short guys. Someone 5'8" with short femurs will always squat "prettier." Your mechanics are different and that's fine — you'll still build big legs.

💪 Long Arms

Your wingspan is proportionally long for your frame. This affects pressing and pulling.
  • Bench press: Longer range of motion = harder. Don't compare your bench numbers to short-armed guys. Your chest will still grow, it just takes more work per rep.
  • Deadlifts & rows: This is your ADVANTAGE. Long arms = less distance to pull the bar off the floor. You're mechanically built to deadlift heavy. Lean into it.
  • Pull-ups/chin-ups: Harder due to longer range of motion. Start with lat pulldowns if needed, work toward bodyweight pull-ups, then add weight.
  • Curls: Long bicep bellies have huge growth potential. When your arms fill out, they'll look great because there's more surface area.

🌱 Ectomorph Frame — Your Advantage

You're lean at 140 with visible shoulder definition already. That's a great starting point.
  • You'll stay lean while gaining. Your metabolism is fast, which means the 4K calories will go toward muscle, not fat — as long as you're training hard.
  • Muscle shows faster on lean frames. At 155 lbs you'll already look noticeably different. By 170 you'll look like a different person.
  • Your shoulders are wide relative to your waist. This is the V-taper everyone wants. When you add chest, back, and shoulder mass, it'll look really good on your frame.
  • The challenge: Eating enough. Your appetite will fight you. That's why the meal plan is structured with 6 meals and calorie-dense foods. Treat eating like training — it's not optional.

Rules to Live By

Follow these and the results will come. Break them and you'll stay 140.

Training Rules
1
Track every workout. Write down the weight and reps for each set. What gets measured gets managed.
2
Progressive overload. If you hit the top of the rep range on all sets, go up in weight next session.
3
Deload every 5th week. Drop all weights by 40% and do the same reps. Your body needs recovery to grow.
4
If you miss a rep target two sessions in a row, stay at the same weight — don't go backward.
Nutrition Rules
1
Never skip a meal. Set alarms if you have to. Missing meals is the #1 reason skinny guys stay skinny.
2
Eat even when you're not hungry. You're 140 lbs — your appetite is lying to you.
3
Protein at every meal. No exceptions. Aim for 30g+ per meal minimum.
4
Meal prep on Sunday. Cook rice, chicken, and potatoes in bulk. Makes the week effortless.
5
Drink a gallon of water a day on top of the milk.
Weight Tracking
1
Weigh yourself every morning, same time, after using the bathroom. Track the weekly average, not daily swings.
2
Target: 0.5–0.75 lbs per week. That's 2–3 lbs per month of mostly lean mass.
3
If your average isn't going up after 2 weeks, add 250 more calories per day.
4
Timeline to 170 lbs: ~10–15 months of consistent training and eating. This is a marathon, not a sprint.
